The Lonely Litle Kissing Gate

by James Baskett
published on May 11, 2020

Kissing Gates are sociable little things, always ready to welcome the next passing traveller, sending each on their way with a little bit of love but pity the gate who sees no one for days, who sits alone waiting through countless hours. This poems is a little tribute to all the lonely little kissing gates who spend more time waiting and wondering when they will next be able to share a little bit of love. 

 

 

There stood a little kissing gate,

Alone amid a moor.

To be a lonely kissing gate,

Is something of a bore.

She couldn't get much kissing done,

And waiting's really not that fun.

Poor lonely little kissing gate,

Alone amid a moor.
